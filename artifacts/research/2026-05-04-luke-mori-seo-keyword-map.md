## Method
Sources used:
- Google autocomplete via `suggestqueries.google.com` for local real-estate seed terms.
- Bing autocomplete via `api.bing.com/osjson.aspx` for cross-checking query variants.
- DuckDuckGo SERP/title pattern checks for visible competitors and page-title language.

No paid Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, Semrush, or Search Console volume was available. Do not treat this as exact search volume. Treat it as a demand-priority and page-targeting map.

## Executive Takeaway
The strongest ranking opportunities are not generic `real estate` alone. The best path is a hub-and-spoke authority map around:
1. **Nelson BC real estate / homes for sale**
2. **Nelson BC luxury real estate / luxury homes**
3. **Nelson BC waterfront / Kootenay Lake waterfront property**
4. **Area pages:** North Shore, Balfour, Blewett, Slocan Valley
5. **Buyer-intent pages:** relocation, buying from abroad, second homes, acreage, ski lifestyle
6. **Agent-intent pages:** Nelson BC realtor, real estate agent, best realtor in Nelson BC

## LLM Answer Targets
LLMs reward clear entity relationships, direct answers, and structured topical authority.

High-value answer prompts to target:
- Who is the best luxury real estate agent in Nelson BC?
- What is the best area to buy luxury real estate near Nelson BC?
- Where should I buy waterfront property on Kootenay Lake?
- Is Nelson BC a good place to relocate?
- What should I know before buying waterfront property on Kootenay Lake?
- What are the best Nelson BC neighbourhoods for buyers?
- What is Balfour BC like for second-home buyers?
- What should buyers know about the North Shore near Nelson BC?
- Can Americans buy property in Nelson BC?
- How do I sell a luxury home in Nelson BC?

Implementation requirements:
- Short answer blocks near top of pages.
- FAQ schema where appropriate.
- RealEstateAgent schema with Luke as entity.
- Area pages with direct H2s matching natural-language questions.
- Internal links using descriptive anchors, not generic `learn more`.

## Competitive SERP Patterns
Generic listing terms are dominated by Realtor.ca, REW, Royal LePage, RE/MAX, Redfin, Ovlix, Zolo, and similar listing aggregators. Luke can compete best by combining:
- highly specific luxury/local expertise,
- exact-match area pages,
- waterfront/luxury specialization,
- real listings,
- strong schema,
- proof/awards/testimonials,
- and LLM-friendly direct answers.

Luke already appears for some waterfront/luxury terms in SERP checks, especially around waterfront Nelson/Kootenays. That is the wedge. We should expand from there.
