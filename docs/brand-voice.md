# Luke Mori Real Estate Brand Voice

## Core positioning
Clear, local guidance for important real estate decisions in Nelson and Kootenay Lake.

The site should feel premium because it is thoughtful, calm, useful, and easy to understand. It should never feel distant, cryptic, theatrical, or like AI trying to sound expensive.

## Brand personality
Warm authority. Local confidence. Human clarity.

Luke's brand should feel like an experienced local expert who understands the stakes, explains things clearly, and helps people make a confident next move.

The voice should:
- know the area deeply without showing off
- explain expensive decisions in plain English
- make people feel calm, prepared, and respected
- use real local details: roads, water, sun, access, winter, acreage, timing, services, and market fit
- guide without pressure
- sound polished but never cold

## Emotional promise
You do not have to figure this out alone.

## Tone pillars

### 1. Clear over clever
If a visitor has to decode the sentence, rewrite it.

Use:
- Share what matters most.
- We will help you understand the right next step.
- A clearer way to buy or sell in Nelson and Kootenay Lake.

Avoid:
- Choose the file.
- Enter the quieter room.
- Unlock elevated market intelligence.

### 2. Supportive, not superior
The copy should make people feel understood, not screened.

Use:
- Thoughtful guidance for an important move.
- Tell Luke what you are hoping for.
- Move forward with more clarity.

Avoid:
- For discerning clients.
- Private advisory for rare properties.
- Curated access for qualified buyers.

### 3. Confident, not dramatic
The design can carry the luxury. The words should stay steady.

Use:
- Understand your options before you make a move.
- Sell with a clear plan.
- Waterfront decisions deserve careful advice.

Avoid:
- The next move begins in private.
- The market opens differently here.
- Prestige without compromise.

### 4. Local and practical
Luxury here is not abstract. It is water, access, privacy, views, acreage, winter, sun, roads, and fit.

Use:
- Compare road access, sun exposure, privacy, water, and winter use before you decide.
- Acreage takes a careful look at services, access, and long-term upkeep.

Avoid:
- Lifestyle architecture.
- Estate intelligence.
- Elevated regional fluency.

### 5. Human first
People are buying, selling, relocating, retiring, downsizing, investing, or finding a second home. Speak to that.

Use:
- A home search should feel exciting, not overwhelming.
- Selling a special property starts with understanding what matters most.

Avoid:
- Begin your luxury journey.
- Activate your next chapter.

## Headline formula
Simple human outcome plus local context.

Examples:
- Move with confidence in Nelson and Kootenay Lake.
- A clearer way to buy or sell in the Kootenays.
- Find the right home, not just another listing.
- Sell with a plan that respects your home and your time.
- Buying acreage takes local guidance.
- Waterfront decisions deserve careful advice.
- Relocating here should feel exciting, not overwhelming.

## CTA rules
CTAs should say what happens next.

Use:
- Talk to Luke
- Send Luke a note
- Ask about this property
- Start your search
- Get local guidance
- Plan your sale
- Read this guide

Avoid:
- Begin
- Explore the experience
- Enter
- Unlock
- Discover more, unless there is no better option

## Guide rules
Guides should feel useful in a real purchase or sale, not like generic SEO content.

Each guide should:
- open with who it helps
- use plain labels for checklists and questions
- state what must be verified without repeating the same warning in every paragraph
- keep source links visible and practical
- avoid pretending general guidance is legal, tax, insurance, strata, or engineering advice
- make the reader feel more prepared, not more overwhelmed

## Rewrite checklist
For every page and component:
1. Can a normal person understand the headline in two seconds?
2. Does the copy make the visitor feel helped instead of judged?
3. Is the local value clear?
4. Did we remove luxury jargon and theatrical metaphors?
5. Did we preserve factual property details?
6. Is the CTA specific and useful?
7. Does the page sound like the same calm local expert as the rest of the site?
8. Are there no em dashes?
